Why RPM and deadhead matter

Rate per mile is useful only when it is compared against all costs and all miles. Deadhead miles may not generate revenue, but they still burn time, fuel and equipment capacity. A load with a good loaded RPM can still be weak if deadhead is too high.

Use break-even RPM to know your minimum survival rate, and target RPM to know what you should quote to hit your margin goal.

RPM Formulas

Revenue = Loaded Miles × RPM
Break-even RPM = Cost ÷ Miles
Target RPM = Target Revenue ÷ Loaded Miles

Rate Per Mile FAQ

Should I calculate RPM using loaded miles or all miles?

Look at both. Loaded RPM shows customer revenue rate. All-mile RPM shows the real impact of deadhead.

What is break-even RPM?

It is the rate per mile needed to cover total costs. Below that level, the load loses money.

What is target RPM?

Target RPM is the rate per loaded mile needed to reach your desired margin.
