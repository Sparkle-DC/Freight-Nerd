What reverse quote mode does

Reverse mode starts with your costs, loaded miles, deadhead miles and target margin, then works backward to calculate the revenue and RPM you should quote. This is better than guessing a rate and discovering later that the load was weak.

Use the recommended RPM as your minimum quote target. The quote cushion adds room for negotiation, fuel movement, hidden delays or small accessorial surprises.

Reverse Quote Formula

Target revenue = Cost ÷ (1 − margin)
Recommended RPM = Target revenue ÷ loaded miles
Quote RPM = Recommended RPM + cushion

Recommended RPM FAQ

What rate per mile should I quote?

Quote at or above the recommended RPM if the cost inputs are realistic. Below that level, you will miss your target margin.

Why add a quote cushion?

A cushion gives negotiation space and protects against small cost surprises such as fuel movement, waiting time or routing changes.

Does recommended RPM include deadhead?

Deadhead is included through the cost inputs and shown separately in all-mile RPM. If deadhead increases your real cost, include that cost before trusting the recommendation.
