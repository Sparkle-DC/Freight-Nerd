Why break-even matters

A load is not good because the revenue looks high. It is good only if the revenue covers all real costs and leaves enough margin. Break-even revenue tells you the absolute minimum you need to cover costs. Target margin revenue tells you what you should quote if you want a specific profit percentage.

This is the difference between calculating after the fact and making the decision before you accept the load.

Break-Even Formula

Break-even revenue = Total costs

Target revenue = Total costs ÷ (1 − target margin).

Break-Even FAQ

What is break-even revenue?

It is the revenue required to cover total costs. At break-even, profit is zero.

How do I calculate revenue needed for a target margin?

Divide total cost by one minus the target margin. Example: $4,000 cost with 20% target margin needs $5,000 revenue.

Should I accept a load below target margin?

Only if there is a strategic reason. Otherwise, taking below-target loads trains you to stay busy while making weak money.
