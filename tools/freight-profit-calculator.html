Profit Breakdown

What is freight profit?

Freight profit is the money left after subtracting the real cost of moving a shipment from the revenue earned on that shipment. For a broker, cost may be carrier pay and commission. For a carrier, cost may include driver pay, fuel, tolls, maintenance, insurance and other operating expenses.

A load can look attractive by revenue but still be weak once fuel, carrier cost, accessorials and hidden expenses are included.

Profit Formula

Revenue − Total Costs = Profit

Margin = Profit ÷ Revenue × 100.

Costs to include before quoting

Carrier or driver pay

The main cost for brokers and asset-based carriers.

Fuel and tolls

Variable costs that can quickly destroy margin.

Commission

Broker, agent or sales commission tied to the load.

Accessorials

Detention, layover, lumper, liftgate, storage and other extra charges.

Freight Profit Calculator FAQ

How do I calculate profit on a freight load?

Subtract all shipment costs from total revenue. The result is profit in dollars.

How do I calculate freight margin?

Divide profit by revenue and multiply by 100. A $1,000 profit on $5,000 revenue is a 20% margin.

What costs do brokers forget?

Common misses include detention, lumper fees, layover, commission, payment processing, insurance, claims exposure and revised carrier charges.

Is high revenue always good?

No. Revenue is vanity if cost is too high. Margin and net profit decide whether the load is worth taking.
